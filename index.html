  1. Invoke the magnifing glass using menu item
  2. Choose a pixel on the screen. Activate Hi-Precision mode for more accurate movement.
  3. Choose a format of your choice using quick filter
  4. Press Cmd+V to paste the format into your editor

New Magnifing Glass

The original ColorSnapper magnifiying glass design, introduced in 2010 and adopted by every modern color picker app since then, just got even better!

  • High Precision

    The new magnifier glass allows you to pick every retina pixel on HiDPI screens, working astnishing good also on non-retina displays by slowing down the movement of the mouse and increasing magnification to a maximum.

  • Accurate colors

    Get the same color value as you authored, no matter what Color Management setting you’re using? Our color picker is smart enough and tries to get the closest possible result.

  • Control with gestures or keyboard

    Zoom, change capture diameter, switch between formats or move to the next pixel. You have total and granular control. And you always can see the chosen clipboard format and its values in the overlay.

  • Multi-monitor and Spaces support

    Use multiple displays or switch between spaces or open applications when looking for the color.

Organize, adjust & export colors…

  • History & Favorites

    Go back in time and see hundreds of colors you’ve already picked from screen or choose some you use often and add them to favorites to get a quick access.

  • Quick formats filter

    Simple start typing first letters to quickly search through the more than 30 clipboard formats including CSS, ObjC, Swift and also exports colors to Adobe Photoshop and Illustrator.

  • Modify selected color

    Do you ever wanted to make a color you’ve just picked a bit transparent? Or probably a bit darker? ColorSnapper2 seamlessly integrates the Apple’s ColorPanel to make it easy. This also means you still can use installed extensions like Skala Color or Hues.

Match your coding style.

ColorSnapper 2 allows you to match color formatting to your coding style. Along with all new suported formats, including:

  • UIColor,
  • Swift,
  • Android,
  • Java,
  • CGColor

This results in even faster developer workflow.
